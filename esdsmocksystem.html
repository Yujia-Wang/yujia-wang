OVERVIEW

USER NEEDS

ESD smocks is a practical solution to minimize the electric fields generated from clothing by shielding people’s insulative clothing.However, ESD smocks need to be managed and maintained carefully to reduce the possible damage. Thus, a fluent and systematic management of ESD smocks is extremely important for related industries. In terms of assigning and returning ESD smocks, the managers and staffs have the following demands:

As a smock manager,

  • I want to easily track the conditions of smocks so that I can better manage these smocks.

As a staff who needs to wear a smock,

  • I want to quickly receive and return the smock so that I can focus more on my real work.
  • I want to make sure the smock is in a good condition so that I don’t need to worry about my safety.

SOLUTION

Here I present two different platforms of applications: kiosk application and tablet application. These two platforms share similar functions and flows, but are different in certain aspects.

Kiosk Application

  • Compared with the tablet application, the interface is more simple and straightforward as most people expect.
  • It's more suitable to tackle simple tasks.

Tablet Application

  • It can do much more than just assigning and returning the smocks. In this tablet application, the assigning and returning function is only a part of a comprehensive management system. People are more comfortable to use tablets to do the management work.
  • It's more flexible due to its portability.

RESEARCH

WHY AM I BUILDING THIS?

Electrostatic discharge (ESD) can make electronic components fail, erase or alter magnetic media, destroy sensitive electronics, even cause fires and explosions. In industries that are sensitive to electrostatic dust and have strict requirements for cleanliness, ESD smocks is a practical solution to minimize the electric fields generated from clothing by shielding people’s insulative clothing. However, ESD smocks need to be managed and maintained carefully to reduce the possible damage. Thus, a fluent and systematic management of ESD smocks is extremely important for related industries.

WHO AM I BUILDING IT FOR?

ESD Smock System includes two general categories of people: ESD smock managers and staffs who need to wear ESD smocks.

WHEN AND WHERE WILL IT BE USED?

After defining the target user group, I need to better understand their context and problems when they go through this process. Since I don’t have the access to the real users, I listed the steps that they need to go through when they assign and return smocks. Then for each step, I thought about the problems they may encounter.

Assign the smock

  • The staff approaches to the ESD smock pick-up area. - The staff may need to wait if there’s a waiting line.
  • The staff scans the ID badge.
  • The manager checks the ID information and the smock size that the staff needs.
  • The manager fetches the smock. - The manager may forget the size they should fetch on their way.
  • The manager scans the smock barcode.
  • The manager double-checks the smock information. - It’s essential to double-check due to safety reasons.
  • The manager gives the smock to the staff and the staff leaves.

Return the smock

  • The staff approaches to the ESD smock pick-up area. - The staff may need to wait if there’s a waiting line.
  • The staff gives the smock to the manager and leaves.
  • The manager scans the smock barcode.
  • The manager checks the condition of the smock.
  • The manager puts the smock in to the corresponding area. - They may make mistakes by putting the smocks in the wrong area.

With some other considerations, I established the following user needs:

As a smock manager,

  • I want to easily track the conditions of smocks so that I can better manage these smocks.

As a staff who needs to wear a smock,

  • I want to quickly receive and return the smock so that I can focus more on my real work.
  • I want to make sure the smock is in a good condition so that I don’t need to worry about my safety.

WHAT AM I BUILDING?

Both kiosk application and tablet application can serve the user needs. However, there are some pros and cons of each platform in terms of certain scenarios.

Kiosk Application

  • Compared with tablets, kiosks usually have a larger screen to draw user’s attention.
  • Although kiosks need to extra external devices to achieve the functionalities such as reading ID cards, it can come equipped with more devices to achieve special functionalities that tablets can’t provide, since a tablet can usually be connected with one external device.

Tablet Application

  • A tablet is much cheaper than a kiosk. The price of traditional display kiosks can range from $5000 to $10,000 and higher.
  • The size of the tablet determines it has a great portability. Relatively, the kiosk usually takes more space.
  • From the perspective of new users, they are probably more familiar with tablets and are more comfortable using them.
  • Tablets’ built-in cameras can be coaxed into performing functions such as reading ID cards or barcodes.

DESIGN

IDEATION & SKETCHING

Based on the steps the managers and the staffs have to go through, I sketched the interfaces first by following the process.

Kiosk Application

The kiosk application design focuses on ease of use and efficiency. Since most people's experience with kiosks are usually simple and quick, to cater their expectations, my design also follows this pattern. It only tackles the tasks of assigning and returning the smocks.

Tablet Application

I assumed the managers are also in charge of other management instead of only assigning and returning the smocks. In this case, an application that only tackles this task seems not very useful. Thus, I integrated this part as a function of a big management platform so that the managers can also do other types of management. And I also used popup windows as the main operating area because it's more simple and intuitive for users.

After I finished the sketches, I asked several experts for heuristic evaluation.

HOW COULD I MEASURE IT?

To measure the success of these interfaces and its future iterations, I'd track:

  • Assign and return success rate - how many users finish the assign and return process?
  • Assign and return completion time - how long does it take to assign and return the smocks?
